Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

Dynamic Equilibrium ("we," "us," "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or engage with our services.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

When you contact us or engage our services, we may collect:

  • Name and contact details (email, phone number)
  • Professional information (company, role)
  • Communication preferences and language
  • Health information provided during consultations (stored securely and separately)

Automated Information

We collect certain information automatically when you visit our website:

  • IP address and geographic location
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages visited and time spent on site
  • Referral source

2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your inquiries and provide requested services
  • To schedule and conduct consultations and therapy sessions
  • To maintain medical records and treatment documentation
  • To improve our website and services
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • To send service-related communications (with your consent)

3. Medical Confidentiality

Therapeutic Privilege: All health information shared during consultations is protected by medical confidentiality and professional ethics codes. We maintain strict security measures to protect therapeutic communications and clinical records.

Health information is stored in secure, encrypted systems separate from general business records and is accessible only to authorized clinical personnel.

4. Data Protection Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information:

  • Encrypted data transmission (SSL/TLS)
  • Secure storage systems with access controls
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Staff training on data protection and confidentiality
  • Secure communication channels for sensitive information

5. Third-Party Services

We use select third-party services to operate our website and deliver services:

  • Analytics: Google Analytics (anonymized data) to understand website usage
  • Forms: Formspree for contact form processing
  • Communications: Secure messaging platforms (Signal, Telegram) as agreed with clients

These providers are carefully selected and bound by confidentiality agreements. They process data only as instructed and implement appropriate security measures.

6. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses minimal cookies:

  • Essential cookies: For basic website functionality
  • Analytics cookies: To understand how visitors use our site (Google Analytics)
  • Preference cookies: To remember your theme preference (light/dark mode)

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

7. Your Rights

Under GDPR and applicable data protection laws, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Restriction: Limit how we process your data
  • Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Objection: Object to certain types of processing

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information below.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide our services and fulfill contractual obligations
  • Comply with legal, regulatory, and professional requirements
  • Maintain medical records as required by law (typically 10 years for clinical records)

Non-essential data is deleted upon request or when no longer needed.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your jurisdiction. We ensure such transfers comply with applicable data protection laws through appropriate safeguards (standard contractual clauses, adequacy decisions).

10. Children's Privacy

Our services are intended for adults. We do not knowingly collect information from individuals under 18 years of age.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. Continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

12. Contact Information

For questions about this Privacy Policy or to exercise your data protection rights, please reach out through the form on the website.

13. Supervisory Authority

If you believe your data protection rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

This policy complies with GDPR (EU), UK GDPR, and applicable international data protection regulations. For jurisdiction-specific information, please contact us.
